The "Lonely Reader" Paradox

We buy books to build an identity, but that data dies on the shelf.

  • The Data Silo: An average reader owns hundreds of books, but this "intellectual profile" is fragmented across offline shelves, Amazon purchase history, and Audible libraries. It is invisible to the world.
  • No Contextual Home: Goodreads is a database for reviews, not a place for real-time conversation. Social media (Instagram/TikTok) focuses on aesthetics, not the ideas inside the book.
  • The Disconnect: There is currently no digital space to find people who think like you, grounded in verified ownership of the same knowledge.

Part 1: The Utility

Instant Digitization & Reader DNA

We start by offering a best-in-class utility that makes digitization effortless.

  • Gemini Vision Engine: Users simply snap a photo of a messy bookshelf. Our AI identifies titles, authors, and genres in seconds with 99% accuracy. No manual entry required.
  • Reader DNA: We don't just list books; we analyze them. The system generates a psychological profile (e.g., "The Stoic Entrepreneur") based on the user's collection.
  • Unified Dashboard: A single place for physical books, Kindle exports, and audiobooks. The chaotic library becomes structured data.

Libby is not just a search bar. She is an AI companion with long-term memory.
