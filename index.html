The "Lonely Reader" Paradox

We buy books to build an identity, but that data currently dies on the shelf.

  • Dead Data: Thousands of books owned, but our "intellectual profile" remains locked in offline shelves or digital silos (Kindle, Audible). It is invisible to the world.
  • No Contextual Home: Goodreads is a database for ratings, not a place for real-time conversation. Social media focuses on aesthetics, not ideas.
  • The Disconnect: There is no digital space to find people who think like you, grounded in verified ownership.

Meet Libby ‚ú®

Libby is not just a search bar. She is an AI companion with persistent memory.

  • Context Aware: Libby knows every book you own. If you ask about "resilience", she references Antifragile because she knows it's on your shelf.
  • Long-Term Memory: We store chat logs in a structured SQL database. You can close the app, come back a week later, and continue the debate exactly where you left off.
  • Conversational Search: Instead of keywords, you have a dialogue. "Libby, I'm feeling stuck. Which biography from my library should I re-read?"

"Libby connects the dots between your books, creating a synthesis of your own knowledge."
